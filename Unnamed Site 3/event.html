Event From My Past

            When I was 15 years old (2018), I traveled to Canada for an art trip. This was my first and still only time I have ever left the country. We went to Quebec City and Montreal. My parents did not go with me, this was my first time being away from them. Through this trip I feel like I was able to get my first glimpse of independence. I didn’t have a boyfriend, my parents weren’t there, and the girls I went with were not people I was super close with. It was just me, myself and I. Age 15. In Canada. And I loved it! I realized at this moment I like having to figure things out on my own. I love experiencing new things. I remember touring somewhere new everyday and feeling like a completely more independent and cultured person when I got back. (I know I didn’t change completely, but when you’re 15 it feels like a whole new perspective on life has been opened up to you.) It was an art trip so we went to museums, churches, beautiful restaurants, and to the Parc de la Chute- Montmorency which is a huge waterfall. Finally, we of course had to go to a place where they make homemade maple syrup called the Sugar Shack. This trip will always be in my memory, not for the places we went, but more so for the feeling it gave me. Traveling to another country without your parents was such a freeing feeling. It made me realize my passion for traveling and experiencing new things. Although I am in college and don’t necessarily have the funds to travel now, it is definitely one of the reasons I am saving my money, and hope to be successful in my career after college. Ever since then and even before that trip, I have always wanted to leave my hometown and find more for myself. I want to see everything there is to see and not limit myself to one town for the rest of my life. Although I still have a lot of learning to do, I am committed to traveling. Even if it means I will have to travel alone. I believe it is one of my soul purposes to see this world outside of Illinois. It brings me so much joy to expand and grow and experience new places, people, and things.
“I believe it is one of my soul purposes to see this world outside of Illinois. It brings me so much joy to expand and grow and experience new places, people, and things.”
“Going to Canada at 15 taught me that being independent can be exciting, not scary.”
